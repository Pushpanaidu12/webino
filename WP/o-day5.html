AVALANCHE LAKE

an Avalanche(also called snowside or snowslip) is a rapid flow of snow down a sloping surface. Avalanche are typically triggered in a starting zone from a mechanical failure in snowpacks (slab Avalanche) when the force on the snow exceeds its strength but sometimes only with the gradually widening (loose snow avalanche).

Day 01 : A powder snow avalanche in the Himalaya's near Mount Everest. It is known by the snow of the Lake . After initiation, avalanches usually accelerate rapidly and grow in mass and volume as they entrain more snow.




Day 02 : Day 2 Slides of rocks or debris, behaving in a similar way to snow, are also referred to as avalanches. If the avalanche moves fast enough some of the snow may mix with the air forming a powder snow avalanche, which is a type of gravity current.
